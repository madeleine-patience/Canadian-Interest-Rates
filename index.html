GIC stands for 'Guaranteed Investment Certificate.

When you buy a GIC, you are agreeing to lend a bank or credit union your money for a specified period of time, typically ranging from thirty days to five years. In exchange, your money will earn interest. The longer the term, the more interest you earn.

A GIC's return is guaranteed by the bank or credit union issuing it, making for an extremely-low-risk investment option. If the institution should fail, however, insurance may not cover the amount you original deposited.
